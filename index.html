Frequently Asked Questions
What is Haylage?
Haylage is an alternative to hay. Here in North Devon where conditions are favourable for high quality haylage, we grow the range of grasses as specified on our website. They are all cut and then left to dry for a shorter period of time than traditional hay so that moisture content is around 35%/dry matter 65% before being wrapped after baling and compressed tightly to exclude oxygen. A fermentation process then preserves the grass to become “haylage”.
What is the difference between first and second cut?
There are two cuts in a typical year. The first is typically in June or early July and the grass has had plenty of time to grow and mature; the second cut is usually 8 – 9 weeks later. This second cut is lower yield and higher protein by comparison. Generally, well made haylage is hard to differentiate between early and late cuts, unless you have the relevant analyses. Poorly made haylage is poor whether it is early or late.
Why buy Equibale haylage?
We have been producing haylage for 12 years having started on a small scale for our own horses. We learnt that most haylage varied in price and quality and most suppliers do not specialise; as a result we noted that service was well meaning but inconsistent. To deal with this, we decided that our ambition was to produce high quality, consistent haylage and provide high levels of service. That meant we were not going to be the cheapest but we knew that people would pay for service and quality.

How much do I feed?
A stabled horse in light to medium work needs around 2.5% of it's body weight in food, with about 70-75% of that being forage such as hay or haylage. For an average 550kg – 600kg, horse this works out around 11kg of forage per day. However, this is only a guideline as horses and the conditions in which they are kept, vary. If in doubt, consult your vet or qualified nutritionist.

About us
The Withecombe family can trace their roots in North Devon to the early 17th century. For most of that time, they have been involved in farming. Equibale can trace it’s own roots back to 2007 when the Withecombe’s realised that it was more cost effective to grow their own horse feed on their Swanmoor farm for their horses and ponies. From that point, Jake Withecombe who had a talent for the practical side of farm production as well as a quickly deepening interest in agriculture and technology began a new specialist haylage production and supply business with the help of his financial consultant father, Rob. Eventually, the Swanmoor Agri partnership was formed and the Equibale brand – together with the domain name haylage.co.uk – was subsequently created.
Neither Jake nor Rob were satisfied with just the one agriculture business and together they also formed the business known as AgriCamera.co.uk in 2012. To top that, Jake then founded TheFarmingForum.co.uk, also in 2012. From small beginnings with deliveries of small bale hay from the back of a horse lorry back in 2007, Equibale has grown to become a niche forage supplier for a wide ranging customer base, including some well known names in horse racing, competition and polo.
Our ethos has always been to supply a high quality, consistent product that we produce or oversee ourselves, supported by our own reliable delivery service. Being involved in farming reminds you every day that we are entirely reliant on the weather in the UK but to manage that, over the years we have invested very heavily in our equipment. This allows us to produce and deliver thousands and thousands of aromatic large and small bales annually, mostly to repeat customers.
